Research Experience

2022-09 to 2025-06 | Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST)

Mechanisms of the photoreceptor LITE-1 mediating the response to high-temperature stress in Caenorhabditis elegans.

LITE-1, an efficient short-wavelength light sensor protein in C. elegans, and its homolog, GR28BD, in Drosophila, have been reported to sense both light and temperature. Using behavioral analysis, genetics, and in vivo calcium imaging, we have preliminarily identified that the lite-1 mediated heat stress response in C. elegans play a role in the ASH, ASI, and AWB neurons. Based on transcriptome sequencing, we focused downstream on the MAPK and DAF-16/FOXO pathways.

This project is ongoing. During my postgraduate studies, I represented my lab by submitting abstracts and posters to the 7th Youth Scholars Forum on Ion Channels and Receptors, and gave an oral presentation at the Yangtze River Worm Conference. Due to my outstanding performance, I was invited to be a session chair and student guest at the conference.
